## Section outline (target ~2300–2700 words)

1. **What "reasoning model" actually means** — the distinction: standard model jumps to the answer; reasoning model emits thinking tokens first. Chain of Thought as the core idea.
2. **Step 1: fine-tune to produce thinking tokens** — the format `Question → <thought>...</thought> → Answer`. SFT on labelled reasoning data, often generated by a stronger teacher model and human-filtered.
3. **The catch: SFT alone doesn't make the model *smart* at reasoning** — it just teaches the format. RL is needed to make reasoning robust.
4. **Step 2: STaR loop (Self-Taught Reasoner)** — generate → filter (keep where the answer is correct) → fine-tune → repeat. The "rationalization" trick (give the model the answer and ask it to backsolve).
5. **Step 2': RFT (Rejection Fine-Tuning)** — STaR variant: high temperature, many samples per question, keep all correct ones. More data, more diverse reasoning paths.
6. **Step 3: Reinforcement Learning** — the reward is now objective: correctness, format compliance, efficiency, self-reflection. Different from RLHF.
7. **PPO recap (from Post 10)** — needs reward model + value model. Slow. The OpenAI / o1-era approach.
8. **GRPO: Group Relative Policy Optimization** — DeepSeek's simplification. No value model. Generate K samples per prompt, reward responses better than the group average.
9. **PRM vs ORM** — Outcome Reward Model (final answer correct?) vs Process Reward Model (each step correct?). PRMs accelerate training but need step-labelled data. PRMs at inference: best-of-N path search.
10. **Reward functions in practice** — code: compile and unit-test. Math: check final answer. Format: regex on `<thought>` tags. Efficiency: penalise rambling.
11. **Model distillation: the natural follow-on** — STaR/RFT are self-distillation. General distillation taxonomy: data distillation (most common — teacher generates, student fine-tunes) vs logit distillation (rare — match teacher's full distribution).
12. **Where logit distillation shows up** — speculative decoding draft models, model compression, reward models.
13. **Two practical sidekicks** — NEFTune (noise injected into embeddings during fine-tuning, regularises better conversational quality); UnSloth (open-source training framework with optimised Triton kernels and a no-code UI). Brief.
14. **Coming up next** — Post 12 (Specialised text LLMs: coding and multilingual).
